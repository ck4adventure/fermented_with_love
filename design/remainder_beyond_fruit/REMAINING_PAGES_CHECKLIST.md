## Getting started

- [x] Equipment you actually need
      Carboy, airlock, wine thief, auto-siphon, trial jar, hydrometer, corker.
      What's genuinely necessary vs nice to have.

- [x] Sanitation without sulfites
      The difference between clean and sterile. What to use (Star San, heat, soap
      and rinse), what not to use, and why campden tablets exist and when you
      might choose them anyway.

- [x] Common additives
      Sulfites/campden, yeast nutrients (DAP, Fermaid), pectic enzyme, acid blend,
      tannin powder. What each does, when you'd add it, when you wouldn't.

- [x] Glossary
      Must, OG, FG, ABV, lees, racking, fining, chaptalization, cap, airlock,
      carboy, bung, wine thief, trial jar, specific gravity, Brix, autolysis,
      and anything else that comes up in the other pages.

## Working with fruit

- [x] Choosing your fruit
      Sugar content by fruit type. Ripeness and what it changes. Wild yeast
      population considerations (foraged vs supermarket vs farm). Organic vs
      conventional. Seasonality.

- [x] Acidification
      What pH does in wine. The main acids (tartaric, malic, citric) and how
      they taste differently. Acid blend. When to add acid and when to hold back.
      Testing pH with strips vs a meter.

- [x] Fruit by type
      A reference page: stone fruit, berry, citrus, apple/pear, tropical.
      For each: typical OG range, sugar addition guidance, fermentation notes,
      flavor characteristics.

- [x] Foraging and seasonality
      What to look for, how to identify good forage, the wild yeast consideration
      (foraged fruit often has excellent natural populations). Bay Area seasonality
      as a personal reference point. Ethics of foraging.

## Fermentation

- [x] Monitoring your ferment
      How often to check. What to look for visually and by smell. Hydrometer
      readings during fermentation. Tasting notes at different stages.

- [x] Clearing and aging
      Natural clarification timeline. Cold crashing. Fining agents (bentonite,
      gelatin, isinglass — what each targets). When to be patient vs when to act.
      What aging actually does to a fruit wine.

- [x] Sweetness and balance
      Dry vs semi-sweet vs sweet. Back-sweetening after fermentation (and the
      fermentation-restart risk). Potassium sorbate if you choose to use it.
      How to taste for balance between sweetness, acidity, and alcohol.

## Beyond fruit wine

- [x] Mead
      Honey types and what they contribute. Why mead fermentation is different
      (honey is nutrient-poor — staggered nutrients are essential). Traditional
      vs melomel vs metheglin. Wild mead is possible but tricky.

- [ ] Melomels and metheglins
      Fruit mead (melomel) and herb/spice mead (metheglin). When to add fruit
      or botanicals. Verbena mead as a specific example. Ratios and timing.

- [x] Cider
      Apple varieties and what they contribute (tannin, acid, sugar). Wild cider
      fermentation — apples carry excellent wild yeast. Still vs sparkling.
      Keeving (traditional method for naturally sweet cider) as an optional
      deep dive.

- [x] Country wines
      The category: anything not from grapes or apples. Vegetables (rhubarb,
      parsnip, carrot), grains (ginger beer-style), flowers.

- [x] Flower wines
      Elderflower, rose petal, lavender, lemon verbena. Petal preparation.
      The challenge of delicate aromatics surviving fermentation. Infusion
      vs fermentation.

- [x] Fizzy ferments
      The carbonation spectrum from petillant to full sparkling. Priming sugar
      calculations. Bottle safety and pressure ratings. How to tell if your
      sparkling wine is overcarbonating.
